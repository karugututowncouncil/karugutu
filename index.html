About Karugutu Town Council

Karugutu Town Council is located in Ntoroko District, Western Region of Uganda, covering an area of 87 km². Officially separated from Karugutu Sub-county in 2010 following the creation of Ntoroko District from Bundibugyo District, it attained autonomous urban status under Statutory Instrument 2016 No. 87 on 29th June 2016. The Council borders the Toro-Semliki National Park and serves as the strategic administrative and service hub for the district.

Functions of the Mayor

The Mayor is the political head of the Town Council. He presides over all Council meetings, performs ceremonial and civic functions, hosts local and foreign dignitaries, and leads the Council in developing strategies for the Town Council's development. The Mayor monitors administration, provides guidance to ward administrations, and is answerable to the Council and the Member of Parliament. The Vice Mayor assists and deputises in the Mayor's absence.

Our Mandate

Karugutu Town Council is mandated to provide local governance, service delivery, physical planning, and socio-economic transformation within its jurisdiction — positioning it as a strategic growth centre in Ntoroko District and the Western Region of Uganda.

Our Vision

To be an effective and efficiently managed Town Council that delivers quality, equitable, and sustainable services to all residents of Karugutu and its surrounding communities, fostering inclusive development and urban growth in Ntoroko District.

Long-Term Goal
🎯

Our Mission

To enhance the welfare and quality of life of our community through participatory local governance, prudent resource management, improved infrastructure, and the delivery of responsive, transparent, and accountable public services to all residents.
