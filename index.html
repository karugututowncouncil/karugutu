About Us

A Strategic Growth Centre in Ntoroko

Karugutu Town Council is a growing urban settlement in the Western Region of Uganda, within Ntoroko District. Officially elevated to autonomous status in 2010, following the creation of Ntoroko District from Bundibugyo District.

Since attaining urban status, the Council has assumed responsibility for local governance, service delivery, physical planning, and socio-economic transformation — positioning it as a strategic growth centre in the district.

Bordered by the Toro-Semliki National Park, Karugutu is a gateway to wildlife tourism and sustainable natural resource management. Recognised under Statutory Instrument 2016 No. 87, 29th June 2016.

Governance Structure

Council Composition & Leadership

The Town Council comprises elected representatives ensuring inclusive and democratic local governance. All councillors must be citizens of Uganda.

  • Chairperson LCIII (Mayor) — Political head of Council
  • Vice Chairperson III — Deputises for Mayor in absence
  • Directly elected ward councillors via universal adult suffrage
  • Two youth councillors (at least one female)
  • Two councillors representing persons with disabilities
  • Women councillors forming one-third of total membership
👤
Office of the Mayor
LC III Chairperson

The Mayor is the political head of the Town Council, presiding over all Council meetings and performing ceremonial and civic functions. The Mayor leads development strategy and monitors administration — answerable to both Council and the Member of Parliament.
