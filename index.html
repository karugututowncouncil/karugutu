📜 our mandate

a town council is a lower local government established under the local governments act (cap 243), mandated to govern urban areas below the municipality level. its core role is to decentralize service delivery, promote democratic participation in decision-making, and drive local economic development within its jurisdiction. in fulfilling this mandate, the council provides essential public services such as primary education, primary health care, sanitation, waste management, and local transport infrastructure. it is also responsible for planning and implementing development programmes, including physical planning, land use regulation, and enforcement of building standards to ensure orderly urban growth.
in addition, the town council exercises legislative and administrative authority through the formulation of byelaws, collection of local revenues, and oversight of public service delivery. it manages urban systems such as markets, street lighting, and environmental health services while ensuring accountability and coordination across sectors. structurally, the council comprises elected political leaders, including a chairperson and ward representatives, supported by an executive committee that oversees policy implementation. the technical administration, led by the town clerk, manages day-to-day operations and ensures that council decisions are effectively translated into service delivery and development outcomes.
Statutory Instrument No. 48 of 2017, issued by the Minister of Local Government under Section 3 of the Local Government (Rating) Act, 2005

about karugutu town council

karugutu town council is located in ntoroko district, western region of uganda, covering an area of 87 km². officially separated from karugutu sub-county in 2010 following the creation of ntoroko district from bundibugyo district, it attained autonomous urban status under Statutory Instrument No. 48 of 2017, issued by the Minister of Local Government under Section 3 of the Local Government (Rating) Act, 2005. the council borders the toro-semliki national park and serves as the strategic administrative and service hub for the district.

functions of the mayor

the mayor is the political head of the town council. he presides over all council meetings, performs ceremonial and civic functions, hosts local and foreign dignitaries, and leads the council in developing strategies for the town council's development. the mayor monitors administration, provides guidance to ward administrations, and is answerable to the council and the member of parliament. the vice mayor assists and deputises in the mayor's absence.

vision 2040

karugutu town council – vision 2040
"a vibrant, inclusive, climate-resilient and well-governed town with sustainable economic growth and improved quality of life for all."

vision narrative
by 2040, karugutu town council will stand as a model of balanced and people-centered development, where economic opportunity, social inclusion, environmental sustainability, and good governance are not aspirations, but lived realities.

karugutu will be a thriving economic hub, powered by local enterprise, modern agriculture, small industries, and innovation. markets will be vibrant, businesses supported, and youth and women fully engaged in productive livelihoods. infrastructure—roads, energy, and communication—will enable seamless connectivity within and beyond the town.

the town will embody an inclusive society, where all people—regardless of age, gender, or ability, have access to quality social services. education, healthcare, and community support systems will be accessible, equitable, and responsive, ensuring that no one is left behind.

as a climate-resilient community, karugutu will embrace clean energy, sustainable land use, and environmental conservation. solar energy, water management systems, and green landscapes will define the town's commitment to protecting its natural resources while adapting to climate change.

karugutu will champion a sustainable future, where development today does not compromise tomorrow. natural ecosystems will be preserved, waste will be managed responsibly, and growth will align with environmental stewardship.

at the core of this transformation will be good governance, transparent, accountable, and participatory leadership. citizens will actively engage in decision-making, institutions will be efficient and service-oriented, and justice and rule of law will guide all actions.

strategic pillars of vision 2040
economic growth
  • expansion of trade, agriculture, and local industries
  • promotion of entrepreneurship and job creation
  • investment in infrastructure and technology
inclusive services
  • universal access to education, healthcare, and social protection
  • empowerment of vulnerable and marginalized groups
  • strengthening community-based development programs
climate resilience
  • adoption of renewable energy solutions
  • climate-smart agriculture and environmental conservation
  • disaster preparedness and sustainable water management
sustainable resource management
  • efficient use of land and natural resources
  • protection of ecosystems and biodiversity
  • sustainable urban planning and development
good governance
  • transparency, accountability, and citizen participation
  • strengthening institutional capacity
  • upholding justice, equity, and the rule of law

the desired future

by 2040, karugutu will be a town where:

  • businesses flourish and livelihoods are secure
  • communities are united, empowered, and inclusive
  • the environment is protected and resilient
  • public services are efficient and accessible
  • leadership is trusted and accountable
